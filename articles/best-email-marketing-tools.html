Mailchimp — Easiest For Beginners

If this is your first time doing email marketing, Mailchimp will feel like a warm welcome. The onboarding wizard walks you through every step — from importing contacts to sending your first campaign.

Best for: Complete beginners who just want to send newsletters. The free plan covers up to 500 contacts — enough to get started.

Watch out: Mailchimp gets expensive fast. A list of 2,500 contacts costs $59/month. And their automation builder is severely limited compared to GetResponse or ConvertKit.

ConvertKit — Built For Creators

Unlike Mailchimp, ConvertKit was designed specifically for bloggers, YouTubers, podcasters, and course creators. Its tag-based system makes segmenting subscribers effortless.

Standout feature: Visual automation funnels. Create email sequences based on what subscribers click — perfect for selling digital products or courses.

Free for up to 1,000 subscribers. Paid plans start at $15/month.

Brevo — Cheapest Option

At $9/month, Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) is the most affordable paid option. It also includes SMS marketing — something no other tool on this list offers at this price.

Limitation: The $9/month "Starter" plan caps at 5,000 emails/month. If you email daily, you'll hit that fast. The free plan limits you to 300 emails/day.

Constant Contact — Best For Events

If you run events, fundraisers, or manage a nonprofit, Constant Contact's event management features are unmatched. Built-in RSVP tracking, ticketing, and event-specific email templates.

Downside: The interface feels dated. And the lack of a permanent free plan makes it harder to recommend for budget-conscious businesses.

How I Tested

  • Setup time: I timed how long it took to import a 500-contact list, create a welcome sequence, and send the first campaign.
  • Deliverability: I sent 100 test emails across Gmail, Yahoo, Outlook, and a custom domain. Tracked exactly where each one landed — inbox, promotions tab, or spam.
  • User experience: Had three friends with varying tech skills try each tool and rate the setup process on a 1-5 scale.
  • Value for money: Calculated the real cost at each subscriber tier (500, 2,500, 10,000) to see which tool actually delivers the best bang for your buck.

FAQ

What is the cheapest email marketing tool?
Brevo at $9/month (5,000 emails/mo). Mailchimp has a generous free plan for up to 500 contacts. But for long-term value, GetResponse at $15.58/month gives you email + landing pages + webinars.
Can I switch email marketing tools later?
Absolutely. All major platforms allow you to export your subscriber list as a CSV file. Switching takes 1-2 hours total. Your email templates won't transfer, but your contacts will.
What's the ROI of email marketing?
$36 return for every $1 spent — the highest ROI of any marketing channel (DMA, 2024). A $15/month tool that generates $540/month in sales is not uncommon for small businesses.
Do I really need a paid email tool?
If you have fewer than 500 subscribers, you can start with Mailchimp's free plan. But once you need automation (welcome sequences, abandoned cart emails, behavior-based triggers), a paid tool like GetResponse pays for itself.
Which has the best deliverability?
In my tests: GetResponse 99.2%, ConvertKit 98.8%, Mailchimp 97.1%. A 2% difference might not sound like much — but if you're sending 10,000 emails a month, that's 200 messages that never reach an inbox.
